Cleopatra VII Thea Philopator
Cleopatra VII Thea Philopator is her full name.
In Koine Greek, her name means "Cleopatra, father-loving goddess."
She lived from approximately 70 or 69 BC to 10 August 30 BC.
Cleopatra was the Queen of the Ptolemaic Kingdom of Egypt from 51 to 30 BC.
She was the last active ruler of Egypt before it became a Roman province.
Cleopatra was a member of the Ptolemaic dynasty.
She was a descendant of Ptolemy I Soter, the founder of the dynasty.
Ptolemy I Soter was a Macedonian Greek general and a companion of Alexander the Great.
Cleopatra’s first language was Koine Greek.
She is the only Ptolemaic ruler known to have learned the Egyptian language.
After Cleopatra’s death, Egypt became a province of the Roman Empire.
Her death marked the end of the last Hellenistic-period state in the Mediterranean.
This period had lasted since the reign of Alexander the Great from 336 to 323 BC.
